Daily devotional plan

Read

Romans 1

Key Verse

Romans 1:17
“For in the gospel the righteousness of God is revealed — a righteousness that is by faith from first to last, just as it is written: ‘The righteous will live by faith.’”

Think

  1. What is surprising to you about the people Jesus chose as His disciples? What is comforting?
  2. Of the three aspects of discipleship — being with Jesus, being like Jesus, and doing what Jesus did — which is the most challenging to you? Why?
  3. What are the indicators that a person is not living by faith that Paul outlines in our passage today?

Live Differently

Look back over our teaching, the Scripture you've read, and your answers above. What do you sense God is asking you to do or change in your life as a result of what you've studied today?

Read

Romans 2

Key Verse

Romans 2:4
“Or do you show contempt for the riches of his kindness, forbearance, and patience, not realizing that God’s kindness is intended to lead you to repentance.”

Think

  1. What does “imputed righteousness” mean?
  2. Why is God’s grace the only acceptable motivation for living differently?
  3. What does Romans 2 teach you about the righteousness that God expects from His people?

Look back over our teaching, the Scripture you've read, and your answers to the questions above. What do you sense God is asking you to do or change in your life as a result of what you've studied today?

Romans 3:1–20

Key Verse

Romans 3:10
“As it is written: ‘There is no one righteous, not even one.’”

Think

  1. How does today's analogy help you better understand how God's grace frees us to live bold lives of freedom?
  2. What does it mean when we say that God will only bless you when you are “blessable”?
  3. We say that humans are naturally slaves to sin, just like climbers are slaves to gravity. How does our passage today further illuminate this concept?

Romans 3:21–31

Key Verse

Romans 3:23–24
“…for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, and all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus.”

Think

  1. Why is it important for Christians to retreat and resist the world at times?
  2. What does it mean when the Bible describes disciples of Jesus as “ambassadors”?
  3. Who, according to today's Scripture, needs Jesus' grace? Who is it available to? Why is this important to remember?

Romans 4

Key Verse

Romans 4:7–8
“Blessed are those whose transgressions are forgiven, whose sins are covered. Blessed is the one whose sin the Lord will never count against them.”

Think

  1. How does the Holy Spirit's power enable us to embrace reality, even when it's painful?
  2. What does it mean to resist or “quench” the Holy Spirit's power? Is it possible to do this and remain a disciple of Jesus?
  3. Given all you have learned so far, what does it mean that God “credits” us with righteousness?
